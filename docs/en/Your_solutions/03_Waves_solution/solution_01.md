# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$. If the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \text{ m/s}$?

## Theory

The relationship between wave speed $v$, frequency $f$, and wavelength $\lambda$ is given by the wave equation:

$$
v = \lambda f
$$

When a wave transitions between different media, its frequency remains constant, but its speed and wavelength change.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Rearrange the wave equation to solve for wavelength:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

### Wavelength in Air

Given $v_{air} = 343 \text{ m/s}$ and $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$:

$$
\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

### Wavelength in Water

Given $v_{water} = 1482 \text{ m/s}$ and $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$:

$$
\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440} \approx 3.368 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

* Wavelength in air: $0.780 \text{ m}$
* Wavelength in water: $3.368 \text{ m}$

## Interpretation

Sound travels significantly faster in water than in air due to the higher bulk modulus (incompressibility) of water. Because the frequency is fixed by the source, the higher speed necessitates a proportionally longer wavelength.
