# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$?

## Theory

The phase difference $\Delta \phi$ between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ along the path of a traveling wave is proportional to the wave number $k$:

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta x
$$

The wave number is defined as $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the definition of the wave number into the phase difference equation:

$$
\Delta \phi = \left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\right) \Delta x
$$

Substitute the given distance $\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}$:

$$
\Delta \phi = \left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\right) \left(\frac{\lambda}{3}\right)
$$

The wavelength $\lambda$ cancels out:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference is $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians.

## Interpretation

A full wavelength $\lambda$ corresponds to a complete cycle of $2\pi$ radians ($360^\circ$). A spatial separation of one-third of a wavelength corresponds to one-third of a complete cycle, which is exactly $2\pi/3$ radians ($120^\circ$).
