# Task 05 – Echo Ranging

## Problem Statement

A person shouts towards a cliff and hears the echo 1 second later. How far away is the cliff? (Speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$).

## Theory

An echo is the reflection of a sound wave. The time $t$ measured is the total time for the sound to travel to the cliff and back. 
The total distance traveled by the sound is $2d$, where $d$ is the distance to the cliff.

$$
2d = v t
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Rearrange the equation to solve for $d$:

$$
d = \frac{v t}{2}
$$

Substitute the known values ($v = 343 \text{ m/s}$, $t = 1 \text{ s}$):

$$
d = \frac{343(1)}{2}
$$

$$
d = 171.5 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The cliff is $171.5 \text{ m}$ away.

## Interpretation

The sound wave takes exactly 0.5 seconds to reach the cliff and another 0.5 seconds to return. Therefore, the physical distance to the reflecting surface is half the distance the wave could travel in the full measured time.
