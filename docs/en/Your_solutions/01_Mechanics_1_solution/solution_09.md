# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Which has greater momentum: a 2-gram fly flying at $10 \text{ m/s}$ or a 60-gram tennis ball moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$?

## Theory

Momentum $p$ is a vector quantity defined as the product of an object's mass $m$ and its velocity $v$. To compare magnitudes, only the scalar speed is necessary.

$$
p = m v
$$

Standard SI units (kg and m/s) must be used for consistency.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Fly Momentum

Convert mass to kg:

$$
m_{fly} = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

Calculate momentum:

$$
p_{fly} = (0.002 \text{ kg}) (10 \text{ m/s}) = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### Tennis Ball Momentum

Convert mass to kg:

$$
m_{ball} = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

Calculate momentum:

$$
p_{ball} = (0.060 \text{ kg}) (1 \text{ m/s}) = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### Comparison

$$
0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} > 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The 60-gram tennis ball has greater momentum ($0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$) than the 2-gram fly ($0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$).

## Interpretation

Despite the fly traveling ten times faster, the tennis ball's mass is thirty times greater. Because momentum is linearly proportional to both mass and velocity, the larger mass ratio dominates the calculation.
