# Task 04 – Magnetic Torque

## Problem Statement

A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10 \text{ cm}$ by $5 \text{ cm}$ carries a current of $2 \text{ A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.3 \text{ T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. What is the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop?

## Theory

The magnetic torque $\tau$ acting on a current-carrying loop is given by:

$$
\tau = N I A B \sin(\theta)
$$

where $N$ is the number of turns, $I$ is the current, $A$ is the loop's area, $B$ is the magnetic field, and $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field vector and the area vector (normal to the loop's plane).

## Step-by-Step Solution

Identify the given values:
* Number of turns $N = 1$
* Current $I = 2 \text{ A}$
* Magnetic field $B = 0.3 \text{ T}$

Calculate the area of the rectangular loop in square meters:

$$
A = (0.10 \text{ m}) \times (0.05 \text{ m}) = 0.005 \text{ m}^2
$$

Determine the angle $\theta$. The magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop. The area vector is perpendicular to the plane of the loop. Therefore, the angle between the area vector and the magnetic field is $90^\circ$.

$$
\theta = 90^\circ \implies \sin(90^\circ) = 1
$$

Substitute into the torque equation:

$$
\tau = (1)(2)(0.005)(0.3)(1)
$$

$$
\tau = 0.01 \times 0.3 = 0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}
$$

## Final Result

The magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop is $0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$.

## Interpretation

The torque on a magnetic dipole (the current loop) is maximized when its magnetic moment is perfectly perpendicular to the external magnetic field. In this configuration, the forces on the opposite sides of the loop produce the greatest rotational twist.
