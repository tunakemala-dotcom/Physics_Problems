# Task 08 – EM Spectrum

## Problem Statement

List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of increasing wavelength: Infrared, Ultraviolet, Microwaves, X-rays, Radio waves, Gamma rays.

## Theory

The electromagnetic spectrum classifies radiation by wavelength (or frequency/energy). Because the speed of light is constant, wavelength and frequency are inversely proportional. Energy is directly proportional to frequency. Therefore, increasing wavelength corresponds to decreasing energy.

$$
\lambda \propto \frac{1}{E}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Analyze the given types of radiation from most energetic (shortest wavelength) to least energetic (longest wavelength):

1.  **Gamma rays:** Highest energy, smallest wavelength ($< 0.01 \text{ nm}$).
2.  **X-rays:** High energy, used in medical imaging ($0.01 \text{ nm} - 10 \text{ nm}$).
3.  **Ultraviolet (UV):** Higher energy than visible light ($10 \text{ nm} - 400 \text{ nm}$).
4.  **Infrared (IR):** Thermal radiation, longer than visible light ($700 \text{ nm} - 1 \text{ mm}$).
5.  **Microwaves:** Used in radar and cooking ($1 \text{ mm} - 1 \text{ m}$).
6.  **Radio waves:** Lowest energy, longest wavelength ($> 1 \text{ m}$).

## Final Result

In order of **increasing wavelength**:
1. Gamma rays
2. X-rays
3. Ultraviolet
4. Infrared
5. Microwaves
6. Radio waves

## Interpretation

Understanding this ordered sequence is foundational to modern physics and astronomy, as the physical interaction between light and matter is fundamentally governed by the wavelength of the photon involved.
