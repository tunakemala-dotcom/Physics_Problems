# Task 01 – Gauss's Law

## Problem Statement

A point charge of $+2 \text{ C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1 \text{ m}$ centered at the origin.

## Theory

Gauss's Law states that the total electric flux $\Phi_E$ through any closed surface is equal to the net charge $q_{enc}$ enclosed by the surface divided by the vacuum permittivity $\epsilon_0$:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Identify the enclosed charge:

$$
q_{enc} = +2 \text{ C}
$$

Identify the vacuum permittivity constant:

$$
\epsilon_0 \approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)
$$

Apply Gauss's Law:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

Calculate the value:

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.259 \times 10^{11} \text{ V}\cdot\text{m}
$$

## Final Result

The electric flux through the spherical surface is approximately $2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ V}\cdot\text{m}$ (or $\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$).

## Interpretation

The electric flux depends entirely on the magnitude of the enclosed charge. The $1 \text{ m}$ radius of the sphere is irrelevant to the total flux, as all electric field lines originating from the charge must pass through the enclosing surface, regardless of its size.
