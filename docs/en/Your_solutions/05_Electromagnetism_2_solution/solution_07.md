# Task 07 – Wavelength and Frequency

## Problem Statement

The human eye is most sensitive to light with a wavelength of about $550 \text{ nm}$. What color does this correspond to in the visible spectrum? What is the frequency of this light?

## Theory

The frequency $f$, wavelength $\lambda$, and speed of a wave $v$ are related by the wave equation. For light in a vacuum, $v = c$.

$$
c = \lambda f
$$

Visible light ranges from roughly $400 \text{ nm}$ (violet) to $700 \text{ nm}$ (red).

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Color Identification

A wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ is situated precisely in the middle of the visible spectrum. This corresponds to the **yellow-green** region of light, which perfectly aligns with the peak spectral sensitivity of the human eye under daylight conditions (photopic vision).

### Frequency Calculation

Convert the wavelength to meters:

$$
\lambda = 550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 5.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}
$$

Rearrange the wave equation to solve for $f$:

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

Substitute the constants ($c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$):

$$
f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{5.5 \times 10^{-7}} \approx 0.545 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Final Result

* Color: Yellow-green.
* Frequency: $5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$ (or $545 \text{ THz}$).

## Interpretation

The evolutionary tuning of human vision to $550 \text{ nm}$ correlates strongly with the peak wavelength of solar radiation reaching the Earth's surface, demonstrating the profound link between biological sensors and environmental physics.
