# Task 09 – Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Problem Statement

A light ray travels from air ($n=1.00$) into glass ($n=1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

## Theory

Snell's Law dictates how light bends when crossing the boundary between two isotropic media with different indices of refraction.

$$
n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)
$$

where $\theta_1$ and $\theta_2$ are the angles measured relative to the normal (perpendicular) of the boundary interface.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Identify the given variables:
* Index of air $n_1 = 1.00$
* Index of glass $n_2 = 1.50$
* Angle of incidence $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$

Substitute the values into Snell's Law:

$$
(1.00) \sin(30^\circ) = (1.50) \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Evaluate the sine of the incident angle ($\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$):

$$
0.5 = 1.5 \sin(\theta_2)
$$

Solve for $\sin(\theta_2)$:

$$
\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.5} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.333
$$

Calculate the inverse sine to find the angle:

$$
\theta_2 = \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) \approx 19.47^\circ
$$

## Final Result

The angle of refraction is approximately $19.47^\circ$.

## Interpretation

Because the light is passing from a lower optical density (air) to a higher optical density (glass), the ray slows down and bends toward the normal line ($\theta_2 < \theta_1$).
