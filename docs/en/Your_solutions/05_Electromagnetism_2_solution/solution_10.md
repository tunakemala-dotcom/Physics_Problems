# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement

What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n=2.42$?

## Theory

The index of refraction $n$ of a material is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum $c$ to the phase velocity of light $v$ within that medium.

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Identify the known constants:
* Speed of light in vacuum $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
* Index of refraction of diamond $n = 2.42$

Rearrange the equation to solve for $v$:

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

Perform the division:

$$
v \approx 1.239 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of light in a diamond is approximately $1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

## Interpretation

Diamond has one of the highest natural refractive indices. This severe reduction in optical speed (to about 41% of light's speed in a vacuum) causes extreme bending of light rays and yields the small critical angle responsible for a cut diamond's characteristic internal reflection and "sparkle."
