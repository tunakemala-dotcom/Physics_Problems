# Task 05 – Field Levitation

## Problem Statement

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? (Mass of proton $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$, charge of proton $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$).

## Theory

For an object to levitate, the upward electric force must exactly balance the downward gravitational force. 

$$
\vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_e + \vec{F}_g = 0
$$

The magnitudes must be equal:

$$
qE = mg
$$

Because the proton is positively charged, the electric field must point upwards to generate an upward force.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Rearrange the force balance equation to solve for the electric field strength $E$:

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

Substitute the known parameters for the proton:

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

Perform the multiplication in the numerator:

$$
E = \frac{16.366 \times 10^{-27}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

Perform the division:

$$
E \approx 10.23 \times 10^{-8} \text{ V/m}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}
$$

## Final Result

An upward electric field of approximately $1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m}$ (or N/C) is required.

## Interpretation

The required electric field is extremely weak. This is a direct consequence of the charge-to-mass ratio of subatomic particles; their gravitational weight is virtually non-existent compared to how strongly they interact with electric fields.
