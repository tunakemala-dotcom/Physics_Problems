# Task 10 – Lorentz Force acting on Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire 2.0 m long carries a current of 10 A. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of B = 0.5 T. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is 90°, 45°, and 0°.

## Theory

The magnetic force acting on a straight, current-carrying wire of length $L$ in a uniform magnetic field $B$ is given by Laplace's force law (the macroscopic version of the Lorentz force):

$$
F = I L B \sin(\theta)
$$

where $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field vector.

## Step-by-Step Solution

First, calculate the maximum possible force coefficient $ILB$, which occurs when the field is perfectly perpendicular to the wire.

$$
ILB = (10 \text{ A})(2.0 \text{ m})(0.5 \text{ T}) = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### Case a) $\theta = 90^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin(90^\circ) = 10(1) = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### Case b) $\theta = 45^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin(45^\circ) = 10\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right) \approx 10(0.707) = 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

### Case c) $\theta = 0^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \sin(0^\circ) = 10(0) = 0 \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

* Force at $90^\circ$: $10 \text{ N}$
* Force at $45^\circ$: $7.07 \text{ N}$
* Force at $0^\circ$: $0 \text{ N}$

## Interpretation

Magnetic fields only exert forces on the perpendicular components of moving charges. When the wire is parallel to the field ($0^\circ$), the cross product vanishes, and the wire experiences zero net magnetic force. The force scales sinusoidally as the wire is rotated into perpendicular alignment.
