# Task 04 – Energy & Momentum

## Problem Statement

A 0.5 kg block slides down a frictionless track from a height of 3.0 m. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a 1.5 kg block, which is initially at rest. What is the speed of the combined mass just after the collision?

## Theory

This problem involves two distinct physical processes:
1.  **Descent on frictionless track:** Mechanical energy is conserved. Potential energy converts to kinetic energy.
2.  **Perfectly inelastic collision:** Momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not. The two blocks merge into a single mass.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Speed of the first block before collision

Apply conservation of energy to the 0.5 kg block (let $m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}$, $h = 3.0 \text{ m}$):

$$
m_1 g h = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2
$$

Solve for $v_1$:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2(9.81)(3.0)} = \sqrt{58.86} \approx 7.672 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Step 2: Speed after the perfectly inelastic collision

Apply conservation of momentum. Let $m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}$ (initially at rest, $v_2 = 0$) and let $v_f$ be the final velocity of the combined mass.

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Solve for $v_f$:

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute the known values:

$$
v_f = \frac{(0.5)(7.672)}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.836}{2.0} \approx 1.918 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is $1.918 \text{ m/s}$.

## Interpretation

The initial potential energy dictates the momentum of the first block entering the collision. Because the total mass increases by a factor of 4 (from 0.5 kg to 2.0 kg) during the inelastic collision, the final velocity is exactly 1/4th of the incoming velocity to conserve momentum.
